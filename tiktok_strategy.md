## 1. HOOK STRATEGY (first 1.5 seconds)

The hook is everything. TikTok users make a stay-or-scroll decision in 0.3-1.5 seconds. The hook must create an **involuntary emotional reaction** — a micro-gasp, a furrowed brow, a "wait what?" moment.

### Option A — "POV Daughter"
- **Text overlay**: `POV : tu filmes ta mere quand elle decouvre ton cadeau 🥺`
- **Visual**: Shot 4 (mother's face, tears forming) — start at the peak emotion frame, slight zoom-in motion (ken burns 105% over 1.5s)
- **Audio**: Raw ambient + a single furin tinkle at 0.3s
- **Why it works**: POV format is TikTok-native. The crying face is an instant pattern interrupt. Users NEED to know what gift caused this reaction.

### Option B — "J'ai fait pleurer ma mere"
- **Text overlay**: `J'ai fait pleurer ma mere pour la fete des meres 😭❤️`
- **Visual**: Shot 4 tight crop on mother's eyes/tears, slight slow-motion (0.8x)
- **Audio**: Breathing + ambient, slightly muffled (creates intimacy)
- **Why it works**: Provocative statement ("I made my mother cry") creates cognitive dissonance — negative framing, but the hearts signal it's positive. Users stay to resolve the tension.

### Option C — "Ce cadeau a 49 euros"
- **Text overlay**: `Ce cadeau a 49€ a fait pleurer ma mere de joie`
- **Visual**: Shot 5 (mother at table, watching phone, crying) — wider angle, more voyeuristic
- **Audio**: Kitchen ambiance, muffled phone audio playing
- **Why it works**: Price anchor (49 euros is accessible) + emotional claim = curiosity gap. "What can you buy for 49 euros that makes someone cry?" The viewer MUST find out.

### RECOMMENDATION: Option A is the strongest hook.

**Reasoning:**
1. **POV format** has the highest native engagement on TikTok FR — it frames the viewer AS the daughter, creating immediate emotional investment
2. Shot 4 (the money shot) as opener is counterintuitive but algorithmically optimal: it maximizes 1-second retention, and the "flashback" structure creates a second retention peak when we return to the emotion
3. The 🥺 emoji is the #1 engagement emoji on emotional TikTok content in France (2025-2026 data)
4. Option B risks being perceived as clickbait; Option C feels slightly more "ad-like" due to the price mention in the hook

**Fallback**: If A/B testing shows Option A underperforming, deploy Option C as the backup — the price anchor works exceptionally well in the 25-45 female demographic.

## 3. TEXT OVERLAY DESIGN

### Typography
- **Font**: Bold sans-serif, closest to TikTok's native "Classic" font (Proxima Nova Bold or similar)
- **Weight**: Extra Bold / 800
- **Color**: Pure white (#FFFFFF)
- **Shadow**: 2px black drop shadow (#000000, 80% opacity, offset 1px down 1px right, blur 3px)
- **Outline alternative**: If shadow is insufficient, use 2px black stroke around white text

### Positioning
- **Primary position**: Centered horizontally, upper third of frame (Y: 15-30% from top)
- **Reason**: TikTok UI occupies bottom 20% (like/comment/share buttons on right, caption area at bottom). Upper third is the "safe zone" for maximum readability.
- **Secondary position** (for CTA): Centered, middle of frame (Y: 40-50%)

### Size
- **Hook text**: 48-56px equivalent (LARGE — must be readable on a 6-inch phone screen at arm's length)
- **Narrative text**: 40-48px
- **CTA text**: 44-52px

### Animation Style
- **Typewriter effect**: Words appear one-by-one, 150ms per word, left-to-right
- **Hold time**: Full text visible for minimum 2 seconds after last word appears
- **Exit**: Quick fade out (200ms) or hard cut with scene change
- **NO**: Bouncing, rotating, or flying animations — they feel like ads, not UGC

## 4. CTA (Call to Action) — Last 3-5 seconds

### Primary CTA Strategy: "Native Save Prompt"

The CTA must NOT feel like an advertisement. On TikTok, the most effective CTAs are **peer recommendations**, not brand directives.

### CTA Sequence (Shot 6, seconds 25-30):

**Phase 1 (25.0-27.5s):**
- Visual: Furin swaying gently on balcony, soft natural light
- Text: `emogift.com 🎐 49€`
- Subtext (smaller, below): `livre avant le 25 mai`
- Audio: Furin tinkle (signature sound), ambient breeze

**Phase 2 (27.5-30.0s):**
- Visual: Same shot, slight zoom out to wider balcony view
- Text: `📌 Enregistre avant la fete des meres`
- Audio: Furin tinkle fading out

### Why this works:
1. **"Lien dans la bio"** is understood implicitly — no need to say it (TikTok users know the drill)
2. **The save prompt** ("Enregistre") is the real CTA — saves are weighted 3-5x more than likes in TikTok's algorithm, and they signal purchase intent
3. **"Avant la fete des meres"** creates urgency without being pushy — it's a helpful reminder, not a sales deadline
4. **The price (49 euros)** is stated once, matter-of-factly, removing the friction of "how much does this cost?"

## 5. SOUND STRATEGY

### Core Principle: Authentic Audio = Trust

TikTok's algorithm and audience reward **authentic audio** over polished soundtracks. Our UGC-degraded aesthetic REQUIRES matching audio treatment.

### Audio Layer Stack:

| Layer | Content | Volume | Notes |
|-------|---------|--------|-------|
| Base | Ambient room tone per shot (kitchen/garden/living room/balcony) | -18 dB | Continuous, provides "reality texture" |
| Foley | Breathing, paper crinkling (Shot 1/2), phone tap sounds | -12 dB | Emphasize during quiet moments |
| Signature | furin.wav tinkle | -6 dB (prominent) | Used at 0.3s (hook) and 25-30s (closing) — bookends the video |
| Emotional | Mother's breath catch / sniffle (Shot 4/5) | -8 dB | The most important audio moment — this is the "audio money shot" |
| Music | NONE or 2-3 piano notes (see below) | -24 dB (barely audible) | Subliminal only |

### The Piano Question:

**Recommendation: NO piano. Keep 100% raw audio.**

Reasoning:
1. Any piano, even 2-3 notes, risks triggering the "ad detection" instinct in TikTok viewers
2. The furin tinkle IS our musical element — it's unique, memorable, and brand-ownable
3. Raw audio with breathing and crying is exponentially more emotionally impactful than any composed music
4. If the video goes viral, the SOUND becomes associated with the brand — we want that sound to be the furin, not a generic piano

**Exception**: If A/B testing shows the raw audio version underperforming, add ONLY a single sustained piano note (C4, soft pedal) at the transition moment (2.0s "2 heures avant...") — like a scene-change cue, not background music.

### Audio Editing Notes:
- Cross-fade between shot ambient tracks (200ms overlap)
- Shot 4/5: boost breathing/emotional sounds by +3 dB — these are the sounds that make viewers FEEL
- Shot 6: fade all audio to just furin tinkle + breeze over 3 seconds
- Final 0.5s: furin tinkle rings out to silence (creates a "moment of quiet" that encourages replay)

### Furin Tinkle Placement:
- **0.3s**: Single tinkle (hook — pattern interrupt alongside visual)
- **11.5s**: Single tinkle (when mother starts unwrapping — Pavlovian association: tinkle = gift)
- **25.0-30.0s**: Multiple gentle tinkles (closing — the furin is now hanging, we hear it "live")

## 6. TIKTOK ENCODING SPECS

### Video Specifications:

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Resolution | 1080 x 1920 | 9:16 portrait, TikTok native |
| Duration | 25-30s | Algorithm sweet spot: long enough for meaningful watch-through rate, short enough to encourage rewatches |
| Frame Rate | 30 fps | TikTok standard; 60fps looks too "clean" for UGC aesthetic |
| Codec | H.264 High Profile, Level 4.0 | Maximum compatibility; TikTok re-encodes all uploads regardless |
| Pixel Format | yuv420p | Required for broad device compatibility |
| Bitrate (video) | 10 Mbps VBR (min 6, max 12) | Upload at high quality — TikTok's re-encoding will compress to ~2-4 Mbps, so start high |
| Bitrate (audio) | AAC-LC, 256 kbps, stereo, 44.1 kHz | TikTok re-encodes to ~128kbps; uploading 256 preserves quality through re-encoding |
| Container | .mp4 (ISOBMFF) | Universal compatibility |
| Color Space | BT.709 | Standard for web video; avoid BT.2020/HDR (inconsistent TikTok handling) |
| Aspect Ratio | 9:16 (SAR 1:1) | Verify no letterboxing — full bleed to edges |

### Export Command (FFmpeg reference):

```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 \
  -c:v libx264 -profile:v high -level 4.0 -pix_fmt yuv420p \
  -b:v 10M -maxrate 12M -bufsize 20M \
  -c:a aac -b:a 256k -ar 44100 -ac 2 \
  -movflags +faststart \
  -r 30 \
  output_tiktok.mp4
```

### Pre-Upload Checklist:
- [ ] No black bars (full 1080x1920 coverage)
- [ ] No watermarks from other platforms (TikTok suppresses videos with Instagram/YouTube watermarks)
- [ ] No brand logo burned into video (feels like an ad — the text overlay CTA is sufficient)
- [ ] Audio peaks below -1 dBFS (avoid clipping after TikTok re-encoding)
- [ ] First frame is visually compelling (it becomes the thumbnail if auto-selected)
